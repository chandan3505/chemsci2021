About ChemSci-2021 (virtual)

CHEMSCI2021 : LEADERS IN THE FIELD SYMPOSIUM is being held in India for young investigators in the field of chemistry. This will be the third edition of the RSC sponsored virtual symposium and is organized by The Jawaharlal Nehru Centre for Advanced Scientific Research (JNCASR) Bangalore in association with the Chemical Science journal from December 13 to 15, 2021.

Under the banner of our flagship open access journal, Chemical Science, we’ll be shining a spotlight on cutting-edge chemistry research and giving it the sort of attention that drives scientific progress and makes a difference. We warmly invite you to join us for the series of journal symposia, and look forward to welcoming you at this online event.

This meeting aims at providing a forum to the rapidly growing chemistry community in India especially to the young and early career researchers. The conference will cover many areas related to chemistry including Organic and Inorganic Materials, Supramolecular Chemistry and Chemical Biology.

Poster Session


Present your poster to a global chemistry audience via Twitter (@ChemSci_JNCASR) for a chance to win a poster prize!


Poster Competition on Twitter:

Poster Competition on Twitter: Royal Society of Chemistry would like to provide the opportunity for researchers to share their research, network and engage in scientific debate though this Posters Competition to be held online on Twitter. The unique format removes the environmental and financial costs of attending a traditional conference, and helps scientific researchers share their work and network with likeminded researchers across disciplines, wherever they are in the world. Chemical Science offers exciting prizes to the winners including a certificate from the journal. The top six posters will also get an opportunity to present their research as Flash Talks of 7 minutes duration during this Symposium. Poster presentation certificates will be available on request.

Poster Topics:

We encourage researchers working in all areas of Chemical Sciences to submit their poster for this competition. However, for poster evaluation purpose we would request the posters to be classified under the below themes.

          Themes


  1. Supramolecular #SupraChem
  2. Chem biology #ChemBio
  3. Inorganic Chemistry #InorgChem
  4. Organic Chemistry #OrgChem
  5. Physical/Theoretical chemistry #TheorChem
  6. Materials Chemistry #MatChem

How to submit your Poster:

Researchers interested in submitting their posters should mandatorily register for the ChemSci2021: Leaders in the Field Symposium and submit your poster abstract (less than 200 words) though the Google Form in JNCASR webpage at the latest by 30th November. Please note that there may be a preliminary screening of the poster submissions based on your abstract. Selected abstracts for the competition will be published on JNCASR Website & intimated through email with your poster number. You can then create your online posters and upload them on twitter on the date/time mentioned below.

Instructions for selected Posters:

A suitable theme should be selected at the time of registration based on the content of your poster, but the organizers have the freedom to reassign your theme to a more suitable one. Exciting poster prizes (about 12 awards) will be awarded to all winners and top 6 poster prize winners will also have the opportunity to present their research talks as Flash Talks of 7 min duration during this symposium.

Poster session will start on Friday, 10th December 2021 @ 14:30 IST/: 09.00 AM GMT and conclude on Wednesday, 15th December 2021 @ 18:30 IST/13:00 GMT. Winners from the poster session will be selected by Monday, 13th December 2021. The winners from each theme will be announced at during the session of the symposium. The top 6 poster prize winners will be given the opportunity to present their research talks as Flash Talks of 7 min duration each day of this symposium.

Event Guidelines:

1. Set Twitter profile to public for the entire duration of the poster session event – 10th December 2021 @ 14:30 IST/: 09.00 AM GMT to 15th December 2021 @ 18:30 IST/13:00 GMT.
2. Tag @ChemSci_JNCASR and use #chemsci2021poster and theme hashtag (see themes section above) on your official poster tweet. Your designated category may not be your first preference.
3. Pin your poster tweet so your poster is easy to find.
4. After posting your official poster, we strongly encourage you to upload a short video up to 60seconds explaining the poster. You may also comment on the poster thread and introduce yourself! Feel free to add supporting poster information to the thread as well.
5. Judges will visit your poster which should be self-explanatory (see below judging criteria). They may comment on your poster if they have any query.
6. Posters by senior scientists will not be judged by default. All presenters up to postdoc level will be considered for the poster prizes.
7. Please block and report spurious comments on your poster to the organizers/your supervisor.

Judging Criteria

1. Research Originality 2. Potential Impact 3. Comprehensiveness 4. Informative 5. Self-Explanatory 6. Design

Poster Guidelines

1. Your poster will be uploaded as an image. JPEG, PNG and GIF are compatible with Twitter. Use of Videos and/or animations are encouraged in the poster or supporting Tweets.
2. Maximum image size 5 MB.
3. While the recommended image dimensions are 2:1 aspect ratio for landscape, you can use standard poster dimensions (A4, A0) as long as the content is legible (font size 12-16 for A4 and 50-60 for A0).
4. Use of Videos and/or animations are encouraged in the poster or supporting Tweets.
5. Please be mindful of appropriate permissions while presenting copyright images.
6. Please be mindful of prospective journal policies before presenting unpublished data. Responsibility for the data rests on the presenter.

Rules of Conduct

1. Organizers will not tolerate inappropriate behaviour and online harassment of any kind.
2. Comments that demean another person due to his or her gender, gender identity or expression, race, religion, ethnicity, age or disability will not be tolerated.
3. Review of such allegations will be conducted on a case by case basis. Perpetrators can face temporary/permanent ban from the online symposium. Their Twitter/Zoom accounts will also be blocked/reported.
4. Organizers always expect the participants to abide by this code during the symposium and Twitter poster event. Failure in doing so shall result in a void registration and additional appropriate action.

Flash Presentation Guideline:

Oral Flash Presentation of winning posters should be strictly confined to the 7 min time limit with two to three minutes for Q&A.

FAQ:

• What are the guidelines for poster winners giving talks on the final day?
If chosen as a poster winner to present on the final day, you will be provided with information before 13th December 2021.

• When will the posters be judged?
The judges will look throughout the poster session depending on their time zones starting 10th December 2021 @ 14:30 IST/: 09.00 AM GMT to 15th December 2021 @ 18:30 IST/13:00 GMT. Please ensure that your poster is available for the entirety of the poster session. Please do not delete and repost your posters during this time period.

• What if we interact with participants/judges outside of Twitter? Would that count towards the judging?
Interactions with judges outside Twitter will not count towards the judging.

• Will we be giving poster “presentations”, or will our poster only be judged visually?
See the judging criteria written above. Interactions and conversations may take place via comments. There will be no traditional oral component to the poster presentation.

• How will I answer judges’ questions, as we may be in different time zones?
Feel free to answer questions in your own time; you do not need to respond immediately. Do ensure, however, that you try to address all questions before the conclusion of the poster session.

• What if I want to present a poster, but I forgot to include this information on the registration form?
Please re-register on the website before the deadline.

• How do I obtain copyright permission from a journal?
Most journals have links on their webpage to facilitate this process. Go to the paper from which you would like to use an image, and follow the instructions on the webpage to obtain copyright permission. If you are the original author, you can modify your own figures without obtaining copyright permissions. Please acknowledge the original source in your figure caption.
